Swarm Robotics for Autonomous Multi-Robot Construction

Michael Allwright

Post-doctoral researcher in swarm robotics

What is swarm robotics?
  • A completely decentralised technique for coordinating multi-robot systems
  • Swarm robotic systems self-organise using direct and indirect communication
    • Direct: between two robots in the vicinity of each other
    • Indirect: through modifications of a shared environment
What is the inspiration for swarm robotics?
  • Research into the collective behaviour of social insect colonies shows that these systems are:
    • Robust
    • Scalable
    • Adaptive
  • At IRIDIA, we study the intelligence of these natural systems and apply it to multi-robot systems
Recent work at IRIDIA
  • Eye-bots attach magnetically to a ceiling to form a surveillance network
  • Two foot-bots and a hand-bot navigate an indoor environment
Autonomous construction by a swarm
  • Agents explore the environment, observing the partially-built structures
  • Agents detect predefined patterns of the blocks in a partially-built structure, which trigger construction actions
  • These construction actions modify the partially-built structures in the environment
